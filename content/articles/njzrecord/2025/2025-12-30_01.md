# 成员被解约后，K-pop组合NewJeans面临新的不确定性

**经纪公司宣布已解散这支五人女子组合的一名成员。NewJeans的法律纠纷引发了公众对K-pop行业的关注。**

**作者：** Jin Yu Young
**发布时间：** 2025年12月30日

近年来K-pop流派中最具影响力的团体之一——五人女子组合NewJeans，在其经纪公司宣布解约一名成员后，本周陷入了更深的混乱。

周一，该团体的经纪公司ADOR宣布终止与以“Danielle”艺名活动的**丹妮尔·马什 (Danielle Marsh)** 的合约，这成为经纪公司与团体间持续一年多法律纠纷的新导火索。

经纪公司未透露解雇Danielle的原因。ADOR方面表示，NewJeans成员中的**Haerin、Hyein、Hanni**三名成员将继续活动，而与第四名成员**Minji**的“讨论”仍在进行中。

Danielle和Minji周二均未立即回应置评请求。NewJeans何时，或者究竟能否再以团体形式录制音乐或演出，目前尚不明朗。

ADOR是娱乐巨头**HYBE**的子公司，后者管理着包括在全球音乐市场取得主流成功的首批K-pop艺人之一——七人男子组合防弹少年团 (BTS) 等艺人。

尽管K-pop经纪公司解约艺人的情况时有发生，但Danielle的解约在韩国引发了集中关注和媒体报道。这是因为这凸显了在一个由少数强势娱乐集团主导、高度限制且基本自上而下的行业结构中，管理体制与合约的复杂性。

去年，NewJeans的五名成员曾试图解约，声称遭遇职场敌对环境和创作活动受阻。但今年10月，韩国法院**维持了合约的效力**。

在纠纷期间，成员们曾短暂以 **“NJZ”** 的名称重新品牌，并在香港进行过一次演出。她们还为西方主要品牌拍摄了个别广告。但在法院裁决阻止她们独立活动后，她们随后表示将暂停此类活动。

专家表示，K-pop艺人很少将合约或内部不满公开化。涉及解约或法律和解的意见分歧，通常会在内部解决，然后通过经过严格管理的声明传达给粉丝。

波士顿伯克利音乐学院的K-pop产业专家Ray Seol表示：“这种做法反映了K-pop系统高度集权的特性，经纪公司在此系统中严格控制着品牌、信息和媒体接触。”

K-pop娱乐巨头们一直试图简化和规模化打造流行明星的流程。但K-pop中也存在着更具创新性的团体，包括NewJeans。

自2022年出道以来，NewJeans产生了巨大影响，为K-pop音乐和舞蹈注入了更**松弛、自由奔放的精神**，偏离了许多团体的刻板、近乎完美的表演。

业内许多人将该团体视为BTS之后的下一代全球代表者。2023年，NewJeans成为首个在芝加哥Lollapalooza音乐节上演出的K-pop女子组合。

**K-Pop Group NewJeans Faces New Uncertainty After Member Is Dropped**
**The band’s label said it had dropped one of the girl group’s five members. NewJeans’s legal battle has drawn public scrutiny of the K-pop industry.**
**Author:** Jin Yu Young
**Published:** Dec. 30, 2025

The K-pop band NewJeans plunged deeper into turmoil this week after its management label said it had dropped one member of the five-person girl group, one of the genre’s most influential acts in recent years.
The announcement on Monday by the group’s label, Ador, that it was terminating the contract of Danielle Marsh, who performs as Danielle, is the latest flashpoint in a more than yearlong legal battle between the label and the band.
The label did not provide a reason for dropping Danielle. It said that three NewJeans members — Haerin, Hyein and Hanni — would continue working while it continued “discussions” with Minji, a fourth member.
Neither Danielle nor Minji responded immediately to requests for comment on Tuesday. It is unclear when or whether NewJeans will record music or perform as a group again.
Ador is a subsidiary of Hybe, a major entertainment company managing acts that include BTS, a seven-member boy band that was one of the first K-pop acts to achieve mainstream success in the global music market.
Even though K-pop labels often let go of artists, Danielle’s dismissal has prompted intense scrutiny and news coverage in South Korea. That’s because it illustrates the complexities of management structures and contracts in a largely top-down industry that is highly restrictive and led by a handful of powerful entertainment conglomerates.
Last year, the five members of NewJeans tried to break their contract, claiming workplace hostility and creative sabotage. But in October, a South Korean court upheld its validity.
During the dispute, the members briefly rebranded as NJZ, performing once under the name in Hong Kong. They also filmed advertisements individually for major Western brands. But they later said they would suspend such activities after court rulings prevented them from operating independently.
K-pop artists rarely take contractual or internal grievances to the public sphere, experts say. Disagreements involving contract terminations or legal settlements are usually resolved internally and then conveyed to fans through carefully managed statements.
“This practice reflects the highly centralized nature of the K-pop system, in which agencies tightly control branding, messaging, and media access,” said Ray Seol, an expert on the K-pop industry at Berklee College of Music in Boston.
K-pop entertainment conglomerates have attempted to streamline and scale the creation of pop stars. But K-pop also includes more innovative groups, including NewJeans.
Since its 2022 debut, NewJeans has had an enormous impact, infusing K-pop music and dance with a looser, carefree spirit that veered away from the rigid and near-perfect performances of many acts.
Many in the industry view the group as the next global standard-bearer after BTS. In 2023, NewJeans became the first K-pop girl group to perform at the Lollapalooza music festival in Chicago.
