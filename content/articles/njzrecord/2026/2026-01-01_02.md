## 🇦🇺 澳大利亚籍K-POP偶像Danielle被从NewJeans中除名，并面临超过4400万美元的诉讼

**作者：** Mawunyo Gbogbo
**发布时间：** 2026年1月1日 下午12:09 (KST)

### 📝 摘要
一名澳大利亚籍K-POP明星被其所在团体除名，并面临原所属经纪公司提出的超过**4400万美元**的损害赔偿诉讼。

出生于新南威尔士州纽卡斯尔的**丹妮尔·马什 (Danielle Marsh)** 是2022年出道的NewJeans的成员。

---

### 🔮 未来前景如何？
NewJeans的未来仍然不明朗。

K-POP组合NewJeans的澳大利亚籍成员正被其经纪公司ADOR起诉，索赔金额约**4480万美元**（431亿韩元）。

出生于新南威尔士州纽卡斯尔的NewJeans成员Danielle Marsh，在所属公司ADOR终止其合约后，被从组合中除名。
据BBC报道，ADOR随后宣布将以违反合约为由，起诉Danielle、她的**一名家庭成员**以及前代表理事**闵熙珍**。

NewJeans的其他成员是Hanni（出生于墨尔本）、Minji、Haerin和Hyein。
她们在2022年出道仅一年后，就成为了全球销量第八高的艺人。

**HYBE Corporation** 是K-POP巨星防弹少年团 (BTS) 的经纪公司，也是ADOR的母公司。
NewJeans于2024年宣布将离开ADOR，声称遭遇了职场 harassment，但经纪公司予以否认。
法院判决她们必须留在经纪公司直到**2029年**。

此后，ADOR终止了与Danielle的合约，并采取法律行动，指责她对该经纪公司与组合之间的决裂负有重大责任。
粉丝们在社交媒体上支持Danielle，发文称：“**NewJeans是五个人，否则就什么都不是**”。

---

### ❓ 事态是如何发展到这一步的？

*   **2024年4月**：HYBE指控闵熙珍试图掌控ADOR，并策划完全拥有NewJeans，以使组合独立。
*   **闵熙珍的回应**：闵熙珍否认所有指控，并召开记者会进行了详细说明。随后，HYBE向警方举报了闵熙珍。
*   **成员立场**：法律攻防战持续，NewJeans五名成员全部站在闵熙珍一边，支持她反对HYBE解除其代表职务的请愿。
*   **2024年7月**：据《福布斯》报道，Danielle给闵熙珍写了一封手写信，指出两人之间存在着 **“深厚的情感纽带”** ，这 **“预示了Danielle对闵熙珍的忠诚”**。
*   **2024年8月**：闵熙珍被免去代表理事职务。
*   **2024年11月**：据《福布斯》报道，一项裁定认为K-POP偶像根据韩国劳动法不被归类为劳动者，这对整个行业产生影响，影响到职场 harassment投诉的处理方式。

---

### 🏷️ 重新命名尝试

*   **2025年1月**：NewJeans试图以 **“NJZ”** 的名字重新出发，但首尔中央地方法院的裁决阻止了此举，并阻断了组合的独立活动。
*   **2025年10月**：首尔中央地方法院裁定，NewJeans五名成员均受其与ADOR合约的约束，该合约预计于**2029年**到期。组合表示将上诉。
*   **2025年11月**：NewJeans开始出现裂痕。ADOR宣布**Haerin和Hyein**愿意回归经纪公司。
*   **12月底**：**Hanni**也回归组合，**Minji**的协商正在进行中。
*   **Danielle的情况**：她的合约被**终止**。ADOR随后宣布采取法律行动，并于一天后透露索赔金额将超过**4480万美元**。

作为一个组合，NewJeans的未来仍然不明朗。

ABC已联系HYBE，但尚未收到声明。ABC也试图联系Danielle。

---

### 👤 Danielle是谁？

Danielle于**2005年**出生于新南威尔士州纽卡斯尔，父亲是澳大利亚人，母亲是韩国人。她的童年部分在澳大利亚和韩国度过。

据信，她流利的英语和广泛的魅力使她成为组合中**最引人注目的成员之一**。她曾站在品牌活动和国际采访的最前线。

Danielle也被认为帮助定义了组合的声音和美学，并参与共同创作了组合的部分歌曲。她常被描述为组合的 **“情感支柱”** 之一。

Danielle的韩国名字是**牟智慧 (Mo Ji-hye)**，她曾在2023年为迪士尼电影《小美人鱼》的韩语配音版中为**爱丽儿**配音。

**Australian K-pop idol Danielle dropped from NewJeans and sued for more than $44 million**
**Author:** Mawunyo Gbogbo
**Published:** January 1, 2026 at 3:09:11 AM UTC

**In short:**
An Australian K-pop star has been dropped from her group and is facing a lawsuit from her former label with damages sought exceeding $44 million.
Danielle Marsh, who was born in Newcastle, NSW, was a member of NewJeans, which debuted in 2022.

**What's next?**
The future of NewJeans remains unclear.

An Australian member of the K-pop group NewJeans is being sued for around $44.8 million (43.1 billion won) by the group's record label.
NewJeans member Danielle Marsh, born in Newcastle, NSW, was removed from the group after record label Ador terminated her contract.
Ador then announced it would sue Danielle, one of her family members and former CEO Min Hee-jin for alleged breach of contract, according to the BBC.
The other members of NewJeans are Hanni (born in Melbourne), Minji, Haerin, and Hyein.
They became the eighth-biggest-selling act in the world a year after their debut in 2022.
Hybe Corporation is the home of K-pop sensation BTS and the parent company of Ador.
NewJeans announced it was leaving Ador in 2024, alleging workplace harassment, which the agency has denied.
A court ruled that they had to stay with the agency until 2029.
Ador has since terminated its contract with Danielle and is now taking legal action, accusing her of bearing significant responsibility for the fallout between the agency and the group.
Fans have taken to social media in support of Danielle, posting: NewJeans is five or nothing.

**How did we get here?**
In April 2024, Hybe accused Min of attempting to seize control of Ador, and plotting to gain full ownership of NewJeans to take the group independent.
Min denied all allegations, calling a press conference to elaborate.
Hybe then filed a police report against Min.
A legal battle ensued in which all five members of NewJeans took Min's side, supporting her petition against Hybe removing her as CEO.
Forbes reports that in July 2024, Danielle wrote a handwritten letter to Min pointing to a "deep emotional bond" between them, "foreshadowing Danielle's loyalty" to Min.
Min was removed from her position as CEO in August 2024.
According to Forbes, a November 2024 ruling that K-pop idols are not classified as workers under Korean labor law raises industry-wide implications, affecting how workplace harassment complaints are handled.

**Attempted rebrand**
In January 2025, NewJeans attempted a rebrand with the name NJZ, but a ruling by Seoul Central District Court prevented this and blocked the group from conducting independent activities.
The Seoul Central District Court ruled in October 2025 that all five members of NewJeans remained bound to their Ador contracts, which are due to expire in 2029.
The group announced its intention to appeal, however in November 2025, NewJeans began to fracture.
Ador announced that Haerin and Hyein intended to return to the agency.
In late December, Hanni also returned to the group, with Minji in ongoing talks.
In Danielle's case, her contract was terminated.
Ador then went on to announce legal action, revealing a day later that damages would exceed $44.8 million.
The future of NewJeans as a group remains unclear.
The ABC has contacted Hybe, but they are yet to provide a statement.
The ABC has also attempted to contact Danielle.

**Who is Danielle?**
Born in 2005 in Newcastle, NSW, to an Australian father and a Korean mother, Danielle spent parts of her childhood in Australia and South Korea.
Her fluent English and broad appeal are believed to be why she was previously one of the group's most visible members.
She has fronted brand campaigns and international interviews.
Danielle is also credited with helping define the group's sound and aesthetic and has co-written some of the group's tracks.
She was often described as one of the group's "emotional anchors".
Also known by her Korean name Mo Ji-hye, Danielle voiced Ariel in the Korean dub of The Little Mermaid in 2023.
