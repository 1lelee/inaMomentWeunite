## ⚖️ 概要

全球知名的K-pop女子组合NewJeans在与唱片公司ADOR的契约纠纷中败诉。首尔法院裁定，该组合与HYBE旗下子公司ADOR的专属契约继续有效，至2029年到期。尽管如此，NewJeans方面表示将提起上诉。

## 💬 关键证言

> “我们之间的信任已经完全破裂，回归ADOR是不可能的。”——NewJeans在判决后声明

> “本次判决确认了我司作为专属管理代理机构的地位，艺人必须与我们一起进行演艺活动。”——ADOR声明

## 🔍 核心争议

- **背景始末**：2024年初，HYBE指控前ADOR代表闵熙珍企图脱离集团独立运营NewJeans。闵否认指控，并于8月被解职。9月，NewJeans通过直播公开指控HYBE的恶意对待，要求恢复闵的职位。
  
- **主要指控**：成员Haerin称HYBE的解职行为“完全显示出对我们的不尊重”；成员Danielle指出闵熙珍是“不可替代的存在”，是“造就今日NewJeans的人”。成员Hanni则指控在公司内被另一HYBE旗下组合的经理指示“无视她”，并于国会劳动委员会作证时重申“公司讨厌我们”。

- **法律进程**：
  - 2024年11月：NewJeans宣布解约意向
  - 2024年12月：ADOR申请法院确认契约有效性
  - 2025年3月：NewJeans因“精神与情绪上的负担”宣布暂停活动
  - 2025年5月：法院裁定NewJeans需支付约72.5万美元罚款用于未获授权的演出与音乐发布
  - 2025年10月30日：法院驳回解约请求，契约维持有效

## 📈 行业影响

判决公布后，HYBE股价当日一度大涨7.12%，市值增加约6.44亿美元。这一纠纷被视为K-pop产业前所未有的案例——该行业以经纪公司对艺人的严格控制著称。

## 🎤 业界回声

歌手Kesha在X平台声援：“我的心与NewJeans同在。”Kesha本人曾因性骚扰指控起诉制作人Dr. Luke并试图解约，最终于2023年达成和解。

## 📚 背景资料：NewJeans

NewJeans由ADOR于2022年通过数月甄选组建，五人组同年发布首张EP《New Jeans》。该组合在韩国本土迅速走红，并于次年凭借EP《Get Up》在美国Billboard 200专辑榜空降冠军。多首单曲如《Super Shy》《ETA》进入Billboard Hot 100榜单。尽管已超过一年未发布新作，该组合在Spotify上仍保有每月1400万听众。

## 🧭 专家观点

首尔韩国艺术综合大学讲师Michael Hurt指出：“K-pop经纪公司对艺人公开形象与行为的监管极其严格，恋爱或自主生活都受到抑制。”近年多起K-pop艺人自杀事件（包括ASTRO成员文彬、f(x)成员雪莉）已引发对行业工作环境的系统性审查。

## K-Pop Group NewJeans Loses Court Battle Against Record Label—Group Says Trust ‘Completely Broken’

By [Conor Murray](https://www.forbes.com/sites/conormurray/) 

Oct 30, 2025, 12:40pm EDT

### Topline

NewJeans, an internationally successful K-pop girl group whose members’ careers have been stalled amid a contract dispute with their label, lost a court battle to terminate the contract with ADOR, a subsidiary of South Korean entertainment giant HYBE, though the group says the ruling will be appealed.

### Key Facts

A South Korean court said Thursday NewJeans’ contract with record label ADOR, which is scheduled to end in 2029, remains valid, a year after the group attempted to exit the contract after accusing parent company HYBE of mistreatment.
NewJeans tried to break free from the contract last year in a highly publicized dispute after HYBE fired then-ADOR CEO and NewJeans executive producer Min Hee-jin, with NewJeans also accusing the company of intentionally ostracizing it from other artists and failing to act when the group’s private medical records were leaked.
A law firm representing NewJeans said in a statement it plans to appeal the ruling.
ADOR said the ruling upholds its “position of management agency under the exclusive contract, and that the artist must engage in entertainment activities along with us,” adding it hopes NewJeans will “calmly reflect on this matter,” in a statement shared with Forbes.
The label added it has “completed preparations for the artist’s activities, including the release of a studio album, and are waiting,” though NewJeans reportedly said upon the ruling it is “impossible to return to ADOR” as trust between the group and label has been “completely broken.”
HYBE shares jumped Thursday morning, rising as much as 7.12% with its market capitalization adding $644 million, CNBC reported.

### Key Background

The standoff between NewJeans and its record label began in early 2024 after parent company HYBE—the South Korean entertainment giant behind groups like BTS—accused Min of trying to break free from HYBE to manage NewJeans independently, though Min denied these allegations. She was fired as ADOR’s CEO in August 2024, and NewJeans demanded she be reinstated in a livestream in September, where they detailed accusations of alleged mistreatment by HYBE. HYBE’s dismissal of Min “made it clear to us that they don’t respect us at all,” NewJeans member Haerin said, with member Danielle adding Min is “irreplaceable” and “made NewJeans who we are.” In one allegation, member Hanni said she greeted another group signed to HYBE while at the company’s office, alleging that group’s manager told its members to “ignore her.” Hanni repeated this allegation in testimony to the South Korean National Assembly’s Labour Committee the following month, alleging the “company hated us,” also accusing HYBE of instructing journalists to downplay the group’s achievements in news coverage. In November, NewJeans said it planned to leave its label, but ADOR petitioned a court in December to uphold the group’s contract. NewJeans announced a hiatus in March, citing the “mental and emotional toll” of the court battle, and in May, a South Korean court ruled NewJeans must pay a hefty fine equivalent to $725,000 for performances and music releases not authorized by the label.

### Crucial Quote

“My heart is with you NewJeans,” singer Kesha posted on X after the ruling. Kesha previously sued producer Dr. Luke, alleging sexual harassment, and tried to break free from their contract in a legal dispute that was settled in 2023.

### How Is The Newjeans Case Unprecedented?

The highly publicized dispute between NewJeans and its label is considered unprecedented for the K-pop industry, in which labels are known for having tight control over artists and their public images. Michael Hurt, a lecturer at Seoul’s Korea National University of Arts, said in 2022 labels are “extremely strict in how it regulates the public appearance and the image of their groups,” and that K-pop stars are discouraged from dating or having “control of their own lives.” In 2024, K-pop star Karina garnered international attention for apologizing to fans after her relationship with an actor was made public, promising to show a “more mature and hardworking side” of herself. Multiple deaths by suicide in recent years—including Moonbin, singer in the group Astro, and Sulli, a singer in the group f(x)—have prompted scrutiny of working environments for K-pop stars.

### Who Is Newjeans?

NewJeans was formed by ADOR in 2022 following months of auditions, and the five-member group released its first EP, “New Jeans,” that year. The group initially found success in South Korea, but broke out internationally the following year with its EP “Get Up,” which debuted at No. 1 on the Billboard 200 chart in the United States. NewJeans released a string of hit singles, including “Super Shy” and “ETA,” both of which charted on the Billboard Hot 100, and the group has 14 million monthly listeners on Spotify despite not releasing any new music in more than a year.
